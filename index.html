Understanding Freight Class

Freight class is a standardized shipping industry pricing classification that establishes uniform parameters for commerce between multiple brokers, warehouses, and carriers. It is defined by the National Motor Freight Traffic Association (NMFTA) and made available through the NMFC (National Motor Freight Classification).

The 4 NMFC Factors

Density

The weight per cubic foot. Higher density means lower class and lower cost.

Stowability

How easy it is to stow the freight properly. Excessive length or strange shapes can increase class.

Handling

Ease of handling. Items requiring special equipment may have a higher class.

Liability

Probability of freight theft or damage, or damage to adjacent freight.
