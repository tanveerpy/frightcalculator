The "GMA Standard" Pallet Manifesto

Unlike standard LTL receivers who might accept a slightly damaged or non-standard pallet, Amazon has a set-in-stone requirement for its pallet infrastructure. They strictly adhere to the **GMA (Grocery Manufacturers Association) Standard**.

  • Material: Wood only. No plastic, metal, or cardboard pallets.
  • Dimensions: Exactly 48" x 40". No oversized or undersized "custom" pallets.
  • Grade: GMA Grade B or higher. This means the pallet must have no broken stringers, no missing boards, and must be 4-way entry (meaning a forklift can enter from any of the four sides).

The "No Overhang" Rule: Amazon's robotic sorters and high-density racking systems cannot handle overhang. If your product sticks out even 1/2 inch past the edge of the wood, the pallet is subject to rejection. Ensure your stacking is perfectly flush with the 48x40 base.

The "PO" on the BOL: The Paperwork Hardstop

In 2026, the digital and physical paperwork must be perfectly synchronized. The most common reason for an "At the Gate" rejection is a missing or incorrect **Amazon Reference ID (PO)** on the Bill of Lading (BOL).
Every LTL carrier you hire must have the BOL that includes the FBA Shipment ID and the Amazon Reference ID in the "Reference" section. If the driver hands a BOL to the guard shack that doesn't have these numbers, the truck will be turned away instantly. Amazon's system uses these numbers to cross-reference the pre-booked appointment in the **CARP** system.

The Science of Labeling: More is Better

Amazon's check-in process is highly automated. If their scanners can't see your labels, your product is "invisible" to their system. You must provide two types of labels for every LTL shipment:

1. The FBA Box ID Label

Every individual shipping carton (scannable unit) on the pallet must have its own FBA Box ID. These should be placed on a flat surface, not over a seam or a corner, to ensure high-speed scanners can read them during the de-palletization process.

2. The FBA Pallet ID Label

Once the pallet is built and stretch-wrapped, you must apply **four Pallet ID labels**â€”one in the center of each of the four sides. These must be placed *outside* the stretch wrap. In 2026, Amazon's visual AI systems are highly sensitive to glare; ensure your labels are matte and not obscured by multiple layers of thick plastic wrap.

Hazmat FBA: The Lithium Battery Gauntlet

If you are shipping electronics containing Lithium-Ion batteries, your LTL requirements double in complexity. You must mark the pallet with the appropriate **Class 9 Hazmat** placards and ensure that your carrier is "Hazmat Certified."
**The FC Trap:** Not all Amazon Fulfillment Centers accept Hazmat. If you send a Hazmat pallet to a standard FC, Amazon will not only reject it but may fine you up to $2,000 per violation for creating a safety hazard. Always verify the destination "FC Code" against your shipment type.

CARP and the Appointment Bottleneck

A common mistake new sellers make is thinking their LTL carrier can just "show up" at an Amazon warehouse. This is impossible. Every single LTL shipment must have a pre-arranged delivery appointment made through the **CARP (Carrier Appointment Request Portal)** or **Carrier Central**.

Amazon assigns a specific **"Delivery Window."** If your driver is caught in traffic and arrives 30 minutes late, the appointment is automatically canceled. The driver will be forced to leave the facility, and you will be charged a "Dry Run" fee by the carrier (typically $150-$300) plus the cost of storage until a new appointment can be securedâ€”which can take 3 to 7 days during peak season.
